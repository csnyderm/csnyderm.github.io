What is the Bachelor of Innovation Program?

The Bachelor of Innovation (BI) Program is a fully-accredited degree program offered by the University of Colorado Colorado Springs (UCCS) as an alternative to the traditional Bachelor of Science (BS) and Bachelor of Arts (BA) degrees. BI degrees are not degrees focused in innovation, but degrees that apply innovation to their focus, such as computer science or chemistry. BI students take many of the same major courses as their counterparts in the BS and BA degree, however, BI students also have an Innovation Core, and a Cross Discipline Core.

The Two Cores

The Innovation and Cross Discipline cores require students to take classes outside of their major degree path, such as:

The Innovation Core

  • - Intro to Entrepreneuship & The Innovation Process

  • - Business & Intellectual Property Law

  • - Innovation Teams

  • - Technical Writing

The Cross Discipine Core

Students must select a cross discipline core of: Creative Communication, Business, Globalization, Inclusive Education, or Engineering. Students may not select their current degree program. In Creative Communication, for example, I took classes such as:

  • - Organizational Leadership

  • - Oral Communication in the Workplace

  • - Interpersonal Communication
