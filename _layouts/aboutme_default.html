Hi, I'm Cole Snyder!

I am a graduating senior studying computer security in the Bachelor of Innovation program at the University of Colorado Colorado Springs. Since middle school, I've participated in CyberPatriot, the largest youth cyber competition, and helped teach teams as a mentor while in college. In high school, I also assisted and led various cybersecurity education camps for elementary and middle school students. When I'm not leading workshops, I enjoy reading and learning new topics or coding in my free time, often working on websites, developing my skills in Python, or learning a new language.

  • Experience

    I've gained experience teaching cybersecurity and STEM topics to students of varying age ranges. At the National Cybersecurity Center, I worked as an Education Assistant, which entailed me focusing on teaching cybersecurity concepts such as system hardening techniques. I developed virtual machines that the students could work on, and managed the laptops that were utilized for each camp. This involved managing their Windows updates and software updates, as well as ensuring any files necessary were on each computer.

    I widened my skill set while working as a STEM Club Coach for the University of Colorado Colorado Springs. Here, I would focus on teaching STEM topics, often through after-school STEM clubs located at various local schools. I was in charge of planning and leading each club, with some assistance from teachers to control the room. Working with the excited students was a wonderful experience, and I learned about planning and how to be heard and lead. For high schoolers, we would provide workshops on Saturdays at the university where we would spend a day teaching topics such as the engineering design process, programming languages, and aerospace engineering.

    Finally, I also developed HTML and CSS skills, as well as some marketing skills, through working as a Marketing Assistant for Willow Crest Marketing Solutions. My job was to post blogs and manage the blog's SEO for various clients, especially hair salons and related businesses. At times, the solution utilized by the client would not meet their exact needs for the look of the specific posting, requiring for the page to be manually edited. I was also in charge of proof-reading the blog before posting and finding images for each post.
